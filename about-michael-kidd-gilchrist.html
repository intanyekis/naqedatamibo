Michael Kidd-Gilchrist is a prominent professional basketball player from the United States. He is recognized for his contributions as a small forward and power forward for the Charlotte Hornets in the National Basketball Association (NBA), where he wears the number 14 jersey.

Age & Early Life

Michael Kidd-Gilchrist was born on September 26, 1993, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He grew up in Somerdale, New Jersey, and holds American citizenship. His birth name is Michael Anthony Edward Kid-Gilchrist. His mother is Cindy Richardson. Tragically, his father, Michael Gilchrist Sr., passed away on December 11, 1996, due to multiple gunshot wounds.

Educationally, Kidd-Gilchrist attended St. Patrick High School in Elizabeth, New Jersey, where he was highly rated by ESPN.com and Rivals.com. He achieved success in the 2010 FIBA U-17 World Championships in Hamburg, Germany. Subsequently, he enrolled at the University of Kentucky in April 2010 and played a pivotal role in guiding his team to the NCAA title in 2012.

Career

Michael Kidd-Gilchrist embarked on his basketball career in 2012 when he was selected by the Charlotte Bobcats in the 2012 NBA draft. He signed a rookie scale deal with the Bobcats on July 7, 2012, and was honored with the All-Rookie second team accolade on May 14, 2013. In the 2013-14 season, he secured a contract extension with the Bobcats and achieved an average of 7.2 points and 5.2 rebounds.

Following the team’s rebranding from the Charlotte Bobcats to the Charlotte Hornets, Kidd-Gilchrist remained with the organization and further enhanced his skills with the assistance of Hornets assistant coach Mark Price. Subsequently, he signed a lucrative four-year contract with the Hornets worth $52 million on December 26, 2015. Throughout his NBA career, he has remained a key member of the Charlotte Hornets, playing as a small and power forward wearing the number 14 jersey.
