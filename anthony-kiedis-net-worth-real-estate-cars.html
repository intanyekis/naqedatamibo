Highlights

  • Anthony Kiedis has expensive taste, with a net worth of $155 million, indulging in luxury real estate and an expensive car collection.
  • Kiedis prioritizes family, vacationing globally to pause from band life and ensuring loved ones are taken care of with his fortune.
  • A UFC investor since 2016, Kiedis made a smart move financially alongside musical success with Red Hot Chili Peppers.

Anthony Kiedis is known to millions of fans around the world as the lead singer of the Red Hot Chili Peppers. He and his fellow Chili Peppers are one of the biggest musical acts of all time, and their legacy is unquestioned. They have been a force since becoming primetime players back in the 90s, and to this day, they still sell out venues around the world.

Outside of music, Kiedis has had quite the dating history, and he has also had cameos in films.

With all of his success, and a net worth of $155 million, it makes sense that Anthony Kiedis would know how to spend his money on the finer things in life. Sure, driving a Tesla is not quite as cool as sporting an Aston Martin, but believe us when we say that Anthony has quite an expensive taste.

So, how does Anthony Kiedis spend his money? Let’s dive in and see!

Anthony Kiedis Invested Millions In Real Estate

Anthony Kiedis has invested $30-$40 million in real estate

After making millions with the Red Hot Chili Peppers and forging a musical legacy that will pay the bills for several lifetimes, Anthony Kiedis has used a nice chunk of his fortune to secure a number of places to live. Never one to fall in line with everyone else, Kiedis has some impressive homes in different locations.

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Anthony Kiedis has around $30-$40 million in real estate. Given that he carved out a name for himself in Los Angeles, it does make sense that Anthony would want to get himself a nice place in the area. The site reports that Kiedis has had multiple homes in the Southern California area, with the cheapest of the pair being over $3.6 million.

Not only does Kiedis have digs in Southern California, but he has also spread his wings into other locations. Celebrity Net Worth reports that he also has homes in places like Las Vegas, Michigan, and Kaua’i. Never one to remain stagnant in one place, it is clear that Anthony likes to live in comfort wherever he happens to be.

Anthony Kiedis' Family Life And Vacations

Anthony Kiedis has used his money to travel and vacation all over the world

Anthony Kiedis has some truly beautiful places to live scattered around the country, but at the end of the day, he makes sure to take care of himself and his family.

The wild Kiedis of yesteryear had a completely different outlook on things, but as people get older and things change, they start to realize that they need to make some serious changes. Luckily for Anthony, he would learn this lesson in life.

When speaking with Maxim, Kiedis would talk about his spending habits and how they shifted over time.

He would say, “In the ’70s it was skateboards, in the ‘80s it was drugs, in the ’90s it was art, and now it’s my family.”

Having a comfortable home is a nice way to take care of the family, but taking them on vacation to clear their minds and get away for a while is also a huge plus. Having traveled the world with his band, Kiedis knows of a few places to make this happen.

Anthony Kiedis Invested In The UFC

Anthony Kiedis has been an investor in the UFC since 2016

Even though his music is more than enough for a few lifetimes, Anthony knows a good thing when he sees it, and he is still willing to make some investments to help bolster his net worth over time.

For those that follow the UFC, they are fully aware that Anthony Kiedis is a massive fan. When crowds were still packing arenas for live events, Kiedis was a regular, rocking some great seats and having some fun when on camera.

Back in 2016, it was reported that he and Flea both invested in the UFC. This was interesting to see, as Kiedis has been vocal about his love of the sport of MMA. His investment in the promotion and his frequenting of UFC cards makes his place in Vegas seem like a really wise move. He is one of 23 celebrities to have invested in the $4 billion group.
